The Classical–Quantum Architectural Continuum

A literature-grounded framing of the scientific and architectural theory that bridges classical high-performance computing and quantum computation — and locates SOLANGE™ precisely at their intersection.

01The Focus — A Venn Synthesis

The dissertation's contribution is not any single one of the three mature fields below, but the region where all three intersect: a provenance-complete, enterprise-architected pipeline that carries a problem from classical ground-state methods to quantum hardware, applied to tumour-suppressor mutations that conventional pharmacology cannot drug.

Fig. 1 — The research focus as the triple intersection. SOLANGE™ is defined by what falls inside all three sets simultaneously.
One-sentence focus. SOLANGE™ is the first provenance-complete, enterprise-architected pipeline that transports a non-druggable NSCLC tumour-suppressor mutation (C275F) from the classical ground-state ceiling (CCSD(T)/CASSCF, ~18 active electrons) onto quantum hardware (VQE today, sqDRIFT-characterised Heron r3 tomorrow) under a 21 CFR Part 11 audit trail.

02An Enterprise-Architecture Lens — TOGAF

TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework, Standard 10th Edition) is the most widely adopted enterprise-architecture methodology. Its value to this dissertation is twofold: it provides a recognised vocabulary for describing a complex socio-technical system in discrete layers, and its Architecture Development Method (ADM) models architecture as an evolving, iterative artefact — exactly the Phase 3A → 3B trajectory SOLANGE follows. Mapping SOLANGE onto TOGAF's four architecture domains converts an engineering build into a methodologically-grounded architectural argument.

03Literature-Review Skeleton

Four movements, from physical theory to enterprise architecture to the research gap. Seed references are verify against the literature database before citing.

3.1 · Physical foundations of quantum simulation of chemistry

Establishes why a quantum computer is the natural instrument for ground-state energetics, and where the classical methods break down.

  • Feynman — simulating physics with computers; the original argument for quantum simulation. Feynman 1982, IJTP
  • Lloyd — universal quantum simulators. Lloyd 1996, Science
  • Classical tractability ceiling: CCSD(T), CASSCF, and full-CI scaling — why ~18 active electrons is the practical wall. Helgaker, Jørgensen & Olsen (Molecular Electronic-Structure Theory)
  • Quantum chemistry in the age of quantum computing — comprehensive survey. Cao et al. 2019, Chem. Rev.

3.2 · The hybrid classical–quantum algorithmic layer

The methods that actually straddle the two paradigms — the algorithmic content of the intersection.

  • VQE — the variational eigenvalue solver. Peruzzo et al. 2014, Nat. Commun.
  • Theory of variational hybrid quantum-classical algorithms. McClean et al. 2016, New J. Phys.
  • NISQ-era framing and its limits. Preskill 2018, Quantum
  • qDRIFT — random/stochastic compiler for fast Hamiltonian simulation (basis of sqDRIFT). Campbell 2019, Phys. Rev. Lett.
  • VQE review — methods and best practices. Tilly et al. 2022, Phys. Rep.

3.3 · Architectural evolution: from HPC to quantum-accelerated pipelines

The systems/infrastructure lineage — the classical HPC pole (CARC/ACCESS) and the migration toward fault tolerance.

  • Quantum algorithms for chemistry and materials — resource estimates and hardware framing. Bauer et al. 2020, Chem. Rev.
  • Academic HPC infrastructure as the classical pole — USC CARC and the NSF ACCESS allocation model as exemplars of the research-computing substrate. USC CARC; NSF ACCESS
  • Hybrid quantum-classical orchestration / quantum-HPC integration patterns. verify — recent quantum-HPC integration literature
  • Fault-tolerance roadmaps and the transitional window (Heron → Starling → Blue Jay). IBM Quantum roadmap; verify primary sources

3.4 · Enterprise architecture as a design discipline

The lens itself — TOGAF and the broader EA tradition, plus the provenance/compliance architecture that governs the whole.

  • TOGAF Standard, 10th Edition — ADM and the four architecture domains. The Open Group 2022
  • Enterprise architecture modelling and its application to complex systems. Lankhorst (Enterprise Architecture at Work); ArchiMate
  • Provenance & reproducibility architectures in computational science (FAIR data principles). Wilkinson et al. 2016, Sci. Data
  • Regulatory-grade audit trails for computational records. FDA 21 CFR Part 11

3.5 · Synthesis & research gap

The literature treats each circle of Fig. 1 maturely in isolation. What is absent — the gap this dissertation fills — is a single architecture that (a) is framed by an established enterprise-architecture method (TOGAF/ADM), (b) carries a problem end-to-end across the classical→quantum technology boundary, (c) maintains a regulatory-grade provenance chain (P1–P9), and (d) targets specifically the non-druggable tumour-suppressor class for which no pharmacological pocket exists. SOLANGE™ is that intersection.
